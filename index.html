Common Mistakes and Misconceptions

MYTH: Letter-to-number substitution makes passwords stronger
FACT: Hackers know this - P@ssw0rd is not much safer
MYTH: 8 characters with mixed case = secure password
FACT: Modern standards require minimum 14 characters
MYTH: One strong password for all sites is safe
FACT: If one site is breached, all accounts are at risk
MYTH: Phone notes and spreadsheets are safe for passwords
FACT: Cloud sync and file access pose serious risks

What Makes a Strong Password?

A strong password must be:

LONG

Minimum 14 characters (longer = exponentially harder to crack)

COMPLEX

Mix uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols

UNIQUE

Do not reuse passwords across different accounts

UNPREDICTABLE

Avoid dictionary words, names, dates, sequential patterns

MEMORABLE

Use passphrases for better memorability (but not obvious)

Passphrase Alternative

What is a Passphrase?

A passphrase is a longer password using a sentence with spaces and punctuation.

Example: "My-Dog-Loves-Hiking-In-Mountains-7times!"

Benefits of Passphrases:

  • Easier to remember than random character strings
  • Longer length = exponentially more secure
  • Still complex and unpredictable
  • Better protection against brute-force attacks

Length exponentially increases crack time - invest in passphrases!
